Hypothesis visualisation
Bell curves show the estimated distribution for each group using sample mean and SD. Below the baseline: pink arrows show the actual data range (min–max) within each group — this is what SS_within measures. Cyan arrows show how far each group mean sits from the grand mean — this is what SS_between measures. The F-ratio compares these two sources of variation.
What are we testing?

Accuracy & limitations: Bell curves represent the estimated within-group distributions using sample means and SDs — they are not a direct output of the F-test. ANOVA assumes normality within each group and homogeneity of variance across groups (checked by Levene's test). With small group sizes (n < 10 per group) and marked non-normality, the F-test p-value becomes less reliable. In those cases consider the Kruskal-Wallis test.
