Hypothesis Visualisation In report ▼
Red curve — sampling distribution of x̄ (width = SE = SD/√n). The blue shading and CI bar show the 95% CI of the mean: where the true mean likely lies.
Gray curve — distribution of individual data points (width = SD). The gray shading and bar show the 95% reference range: where 95% of raw values fall. The gray curve and reference range are included to illustrate the difference between individual variability and uncertainty about the mean — they are not part of the test.

Accuracy & limitations: The red sampling distribution curve is theoretical — it assumes the CLT applies (recommended n ≥ 30, or normally distributed raw data). How it works click to expand
95% Confidence Interval of the Mean
CI = x̄ ± tn−1, α/2 × SE   where   SE = SD / √n
This interval answers: "Where does the true population mean likely lie?" It narrows as n increases — more data means more certainty about the mean. This is the quantity tested by the one-sample t-test.
95% Reference Range (for illustration only — not part of the test)
Reference range = x̄ ± tn−1, α/2 × SD
This interval answers: "Where do 95% of individual data points fall?" It does not narrow with larger n — individual values always scatter around the mean. It is shown here solely to contrast with the CI and make the distinction visual.
Key insight: The CI and reference range use the same formula shape, but SE (= SD/√n) instead of SD. With n = 30, SE is ~5.5× smaller than SD — which is why the red curve is much narrower than the gray curve and the CI is much tighter than the reference range.
