Formulas used in this tool

F distribution

The F distribution arises as the ratio of two scaled chi-square variables and is commonly used for comparing variances and in ANOVA.

F statistic (ratio form):
F = (s₁² / s₂²)   (in variance comparisons)
Right-tail critical value:
Find Fcrit such that P(F ≥ Fcrit) = α
Left-tail critical value:
Find Fcrit such that P(F ≤ Fcrit) = α
Degrees of freedom:
df1 = numerator degrees of freedom
df2 = denominator degrees of freedom

In ANOVA, df1 is usually “between groups” and df2 is “within groups”. In variance-ratio testing, df1 and df2 typically correspond to (n₁ − 1) and (n₂ − 1).
