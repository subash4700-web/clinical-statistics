Background — Enzyme Kinetics

Km (Michaelis constant)

  • Km is the substrate concentration at which the reaction velocity is half of Vmax.
  • Clinical interpretation: A lower Km indicates higher enzyme-substrate affinity. Clinically, Km values help predict how enzymes behave at physiological substrate concentrations.
  • Units match the substrate concentration (e.g. mM or µM).

Vmax (maximum velocity)

  • Vmax is the maximum rate of the reaction when the enzyme is fully saturated with substrate.
  • It depends on enzyme concentration: Vmax = kcat × [E]total.
  • Units: U/mL (or ΔAbs/min, depending on input).

Vmax/Km (catalytic efficiency)

  • The ratio Vmax/Km is a measure of catalytic efficiency. Higher values indicate the enzyme is more efficient at low substrate concentrations.

When to use which plot?

  • Michaelis-Menten plot (V vs [S]): The standard representation. Non-linear regression gives the most reliable Km and Vmax estimates.
  • Lineweaver-Burk plot (1/V vs 1/[S]): A linearised form historically used because linear regression is simpler. However, it distorts experimental error (low [S] points are amplified). Use it for visual inspection and to identify inhibition patterns, but prefer non-linear fitting for parameter estimation.

Units in this calculator

  • 1 Unit (U) = 1 µmol of substrate converted per minute under specified conditions.
  • Specific activity (U/mg) = enzyme activity per milligram of total protein — measures enzyme purity.
