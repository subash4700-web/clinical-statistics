PCR Setup — Master Mix Calculator

The PCR Setup tool calculates reagent volumes for a PCR master mix. Enter the number of reactions, the total reaction volume, and your reagent concentrations — the tool outputs per-reaction and total volumes for the entire batch.

When to use this tool

  • Preparing a master mix for a PCR run
  • Scaling an existing protocol to a different number of reactions or reaction volume
  • Converting final concentrations (in the reaction) to stock volumes to add

How it works

For each reagent, the tool applies:

Volume to add = (Final concentration × Total reaction volume) / Stock concentration

The tool sums all reagent volumes and calculates how much water to add to reach the target total reaction volume. It also accounts for a 10% overage (dead volume) so that the last reaction is not short-changed by pipetting losses.

Typical reaction components

  • Buffer / polymerase mix: usually supplied as a 2× or 5× concentrate
  • dNTPs: typical final concentration 200 µM each
  • Forward and reverse primers: typical final 200–500 nM per primer
  • Template DNA: typically 1–50 ng/µL final (see below)
  • Water: to volume

Template concentration guidance

Template type Typical range (in reaction) Optimal
Genomic DNA 1–50 ng/µL 5–20 ng/µL
Plasmid / circular DNA 0.1–10 ng/µL 1–5 ng/µL
cDNA (RT-PCR) 1–10 ng/µL equivalent 1:5 to 1:20 dilution of RT product
PCR product (re-amplification) 0.01–1 ng/µL < 1 ng/µL

Excess template (> 100 ng/µL in reaction) can inhibit amplification. Too little template increases the risk of stochastic failure.

Accuracy & limitations

Accuracy & limitations: Calculations assume accurate pipetting. Small volumes (< 1 µL) carry significant pipetting error — pre-dilute concentrated stocks where possible. Always follow the reagent manufacturer's recommended concentrations; the tool does not validate whether your chosen concentrations are optimal for amplification.
