What is CFU?

  • Colony forming unit. Each visible colony = one viable cell (or clump). Not a total cell count — dead and VBNC cells are invisible.

Why Dilute?

  • Undiluted samples produce a confluent lawn. Serial 10-fold dilutions (1 mL into 9 mL) reduce concentration stepwise until colonies are countable.

Countable Range

  • 30–300 colonies per plate (standard). FDA BAM uses 25–250 for some methods.
  • < 30 — statistically unreliable (high Poisson error).
  • > 300 — colonies merge and inhibit each other; counts become underestimates.

The Formula

CFU/mL = count / (dilution factor × volume plated)

  • Example: 145 colonies on a 10−4 spread plate (0.1 mL) → 145 / (10−4 × 0.1) = 1.45 × 107 CFU/mL

Spread vs Pour Plate

  • Spread plate — 0.1 mL on surface. Volume plated = 0.1 mL.
  • Pour plate — 1.0 mL mixed into molten agar. Volume plated = 1.0 mL.
  • Same formula — just change the volume. Forgetting this causes a 10× error.

Duplicates

  • Average replicate plates at the same dilution. Flag if duplicates differ by > 15–20%.
  • Common causes: poor mixing, uneven spreading, pipetting error, contamination.

TNTC & TFTC

  • TNTC (Too Numerous To Count) — > 300. Report as “> X CFU/mL (estimated)”. Need higher dilutions.
  • TFTC (Too Few To Count) — < 30. Report as “estimated X CFU/mL (below countable range)”.

ISO 7218 Weighted Average

When two consecutive dilutions are both countable:

N = ΣC / (V × (n₁ + 0.1 × n₂) × d₁)

  • ΣC = sum of all colonies on all plates from both dilutions.
  • n₁ = plates at lower dilution, n₂ = plates at higher dilution, d₁ = lower dilution factor.

Clinical Use

  • Urine — ≥ 105 CFU/mL = significant bacteriuria (Kass). Lower thresholds for catheter/symptomatic patients.
  • Food safety — Total Aerobic Count (TAC). Limits vary by food type (EU 2073/2005).
  • Water — Heterotrophic plate count; membrane filtration for coliforms.
  • Environmental — Settle plates, RODAC plates, air samplers in cleanrooms.
