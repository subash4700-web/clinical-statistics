Normal Distribution Explorer

Purpose

The normal distribution explorer visualizes the shape, properties, and probability regions of the normal distribution. It allows interactive exploration of means, standard deviations, z-scores, and tail areas.

Key Learning Objectives

  • Understand the shape and symmetry of the normal distribution
  • Interpret mean (μ) and standard deviation (σ)
  • Visualize probability as area under the curve
  • Understand one-tailed and two-tailed regions
  • Connect z-scores to probabilities
  • Relate critical values to hypothesis testing

Key Properties of the Normal Distribution

  • Bell-shaped and symmetric around the mean
  • Mean = Median = Mode
  • Total area under the curve equals 1
  • Fully defined by μ (mean) and σ (standard deviation)

Standard Normal Distribution

The standard normal distribution has:

  • μ = 0
  • σ = 1

Values from any normal distribution can be standardized using:

z = (x − μ) / σ

Probability Interpretation

The area under the curve between two values represents the probability that a randomly selected observation falls within that interval.

Common probability rules:

  • Approximately 68% within ±1σ
  • Approximately 95% within ±2σ
  • Approximately 99.7% within ±3σ

Connection to Hypothesis Testing

In hypothesis testing, critical values correspond to specific tail areas (α). The explorer helps visualize:

  • Right-tailed tests
  • Left-tailed tests
  • Two-tailed tests

Reject H₀ when the test statistic falls in the critical region.

Clinical Relevance

Many laboratory measurements are approximately normally distributed. Understanding normal distributions helps interpret reference intervals, z-scores, and analytical variation.

Reporting Example

A z-score of 1.96 corresponds to the 97.5th percentile of the standard normal distribution, commonly used as a critical value in two-sided tests with α = 0.05.
