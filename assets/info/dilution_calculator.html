Dilution Calculator

The dilution calculator helps determine how to prepare solutions of a desired concentration from a stock (higher concentration) solution. It is commonly used in clinical laboratories, research settings, and quality control procedures.

Core Formula

The calculator is based on the standard dilution equation:

C₁ × V₁ = C₂ × V₂

  • C₁ = Initial (stock) concentration
  • V₁ = Volume of stock solution needed
  • C₂ = Final (desired) concentration
  • V₂ = Final total volume

The equation assumes accurate pipetting and complete mixing.

Common Use Cases

  • Preparing calibration standards
  • Creating quality control samples
  • Serial dilutions for microbiology or ELISA
  • Drug preparation and pharmacy compounding
  • Analytical chemistry sample prep

Serial Dilutions

A serial dilution involves repeated stepwise dilutions (e.g., 1:10 → 1:100 → 1:1000).

For a 1:n dilution:

Dilution factor = Final volume / Stock volume

Example (1:10 dilution):

  • 1 mL stock + 9 mL diluent → 10 mL total

Each additional 1:10 step multiplies the total dilution factor.

Units and Consistency

All concentration units must match (e.g., mg/mL with mg/mL). All volume units must match (e.g., mL with mL).

The calculator does not convert units automatically — ensure consistency before calculating.

Clinical Considerations

  • Always account for pipetting error and instrument precision.
  • Very small volumes (<10 µL) may introduce significant inaccuracy.
  • Verify stability of diluted solutions over time.
  • Label all prepared dilutions clearly with concentration and date.

Practical Example

You have a stock solution of 100 mg/mL and need 20 mL at 10 mg/mL.

Using C₁V₁ = C₂V₂:

V₁ = (C₂ × V₂) / C₁ V₁ = (10 × 20) / 100 = 2 mL

Add 18 mL diluent to 2 mL stock to obtain 20 mL at 10 mg/mL.

PCR Template Concentration Reference

The concentration that matters is what ends up in the reaction — not the stock. With a 25 µl reaction and 2 µl template, the stock is diluted 12.5×.
