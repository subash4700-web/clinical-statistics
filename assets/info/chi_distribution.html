Chi-Square Distribution

Purpose

The chi-square (χ²) distribution is used in tests involving categorical data, goodness-of-fit analysis, and independence testing.

Key Learning Objectives

  • Understand how χ² depends on degrees of freedom
  • Recognize its right-skewed shape
  • Interpret right-tail critical regions
  • Connect χ² values to p-values
  • Understand its role in categorical hypothesis testing

Definition

The chi-square distribution arises as the sum of squared standard normal variables:

χ² = Z₁² + Z₂² + ... + Zk²

where k is the degrees of freedom.

Key Properties

  • Defined only for χ² ≥ 0
  • Right-skewed
  • Becomes more symmetric as df increases
  • Mean = df
  • Variance = 2·df

Use in Hypothesis Testing

Most chi-square tests use the right tail:

Reject H₀ if χ² ≥ χ²critical

Clinical Relevance

  • Chi-Square Goodness-of-Fit
  • Chi-Square Test of Independence
  • Model fit assessment
