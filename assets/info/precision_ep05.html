Precision Study (EP05 / EP15)

This tool covers two CLSI protocols for formal precision studies — EP05-A3 for full validation and EP15-A3 for verification. Use the Validate / Verify selector at the top of the tool to switch between them.

EP05 vs EP15 — which do you need?

Validate (EP05-A3) Verify (EP15-A3)
Purpose Establish your lab's own CV from scratch Confirm the method meets the manufacturer's claimed CV
When to use New method, in-house development, modified protocol, or no manufacturer data available Commercial method used as-is — after installation, lot change, or maintenance
Design 20 days × 2 runs/day × 2 replicates/run = 80 measurements per level 5 days × 2 runs/day × 3 replicates/run = 30 measurements per level
Output Sw, Sb, St — full variance decomposition. Compare CV%t against EFLM or allowable imprecision goal Pass/fail verdict — observed CV vs manufacturer's claimed CV using chi-squared test at 95% confidence
Requires Stable material at 2+ concentration levels Manufacturer's claimed CVw and CVt per level

EP15 does not replace EP05. If no manufacturer claim exists, or if the method has been modified, a full validation (EP05) is required.

Sample material — QC material or patient samples?

Both are acceptable, but they answer slightly different questions:

Material Advantage Limitation
Commercial QC material Stable over 20 days; same lot throughout; convenient May not be commutable — matrix additives can behave differently from real patient samples on some analysers
Patient samples (pooled) Commutable — closest to real-world performance; best for biological variation goal comparison Requires fresh stable pools; freeze-thaw effects can introduce non-analytical variation; harder to manage over 20 days

Important: You must use the same material — same QC lot or same pooled patient batch — throughout the entire study. Measuring a different patient each day captures patient-to-patient biological variation, not analytical imprecision.

Document the material lot number, storage conditions, and any observed instability — required for ISO 15189 audit trails.

Precision components (EP05)

Component Also called Captures
Repeatability (Sw) Within-run, within-day Short-term random variation within a single run
Between-run SD (Sb) Run-to-run Calibration drift, run-specific reagent effects, day-to-day variation
Intermediate Precision (St) Total IP, within-laboratory √(Sw² + Sb²) — all within-laboratory sources combined. Primary EP05 output

ANOVA formulas

MSw = SSw / dfW      (within-run mean square)
MSb = SSb / (k − 1)   (between-run mean square)
n₀ = effective replicates per run (harmonic mean)

Sw = √MSw
Sb = √max(0, (MSb − MSw) / n₀)
St = √(Sw² + Sb²)

CV%_repeatability = (Sw / x̄) × 100
CV%_intermediate = (St / x̄) × 100

EP15 verification — chi-squared test

EP15 tests whether your observed precision is consistent with the manufacturer's claim at 95% confidence:

χ² = (n − 1) × S²observed / S²claimed

PASS if χ² ≤ χ²(0.95, df). A FAIL means the observed CV exceeds the manufacturer's claim at the 5% significance level — investigate before releasing the method.

EFLM biological variation goals (EP05 only)

When validating (EP05), you can compare your CV%t against EFLM biological variation goals:

Allowable CV ≤ f × 0.5 × CVᵢ

Where CVᵢ is within-subject biological variation and f = 0.25 (Optimal), 0.50 (Desirable), 0.75 (Minimum).

Accuracy & limitations

Accuracy & limitations: Results are only valid if the study design is followed correctly — fewer days or runs reduces the reliability of variance component estimates. CV estimates have wide uncertainty at small sample sizes; always report the 95% CI. EFLM goals are updated periodically — verify against the current EFLM database for regulatory submissions. This tool is intended for planning, verification, and education; always follow your laboratory's accreditation requirements for formal validation documentation.
