Precision (EP05 / EP15)

This tool implements the CLSI EP05-A3 and EP15-A3 protocols for formal precision verification during method validation. It is distinct from the Quick Precision / QC tool: EP05 uses a structured multi-day, multi-run design and yields regulatory-grade repeatability and reproducibility estimates.

When to use this tool

  • New analyser or method release into clinical service
  • Reagent lot verification against manufacturer's claimed precision
  • Annual or post-maintenance precision requalification
  • Comparing your laboratory's performance against EFLM biological variation goals

Sample material — patient samples or QC material?

Both are acceptable for EP05, but they answer slightly different questions:

Material Advantage Limitation
Commercial QC material Stable over 20 days; same lot throughout; convenient May not be commutable — matrix additives can behave differently from real patient samples on some analysers
Patient samples (pooled, fresh) Commutable — closest to real-world performance; best for biological variation goal comparison Requires fresh stable pools; freeze-thaw effects can introduce non-analytical variation; harder to manage over 20 days

Practical rule: Use commercial QC material for routine validation and ISO 15189 documentation. Use fresh patient samples or certified reference materials when verifying performance against biological variation goals or when commutability is a known concern. Always use at least 2 concentration levels covering the clinical decision range, regardless of material type.

Important: You must use the same material — same QC lot or same pooled patient batch — throughout all 20 days. Measuring a different patient each day does not work: you would be capturing patient-to-patient biological variation, not the analyser's analytical imprecision. The whole point of EP05 is to keep the concentration fixed and let only the instrument vary.

Document the material lot number, storage conditions, and any observed instability in your validation report — required for ISO 15189 audit trails.

EP05-A3 study design

The full EP05 protocol requires 20 days × 2 runs/day × 2 replicates/run per material level. EP15 is a shorter verification study (5 days × 2 runs × 3 replicates) designed to confirm that a method performs within the manufacturer's claimed CV — not to establish a new CV from scratch.

Use at least 2 concentration levels covering the clinically relevant measuring range. Each level is analysed independently — precision typically varies across the range.

Precision components

Component Also called Captures
Repeatability (Sr) Within-run, within-day Short-term random variation within a single run
Between-run SD (Sb) Run-to-run Calibration drift, run-specific reagent effects. When runs span multiple days, day-to-day variation is also absorbed into Sb
Intermediate Precision (St) Total IP, within-laboratory √(Sr² + Sb²) — all within-laboratory sources combined. Primary EP05 output — compare CVt against your allowable imprecision goal

Variance components & Intermediate Precision formulas

The nested ANOVA partitions total variance into its components:

S²total = S²repeatability + S²between-run + S²between-day

ANOVA calculation steps:

MSw = SSw / dfW      (within-run mean square)
MSb = SSb / (k − 1)   (between-run mean square)
n₀ = effective replicates per run (harmonic mean)

Sr = √MSw                    (repeatability SD)
Sb = √max(0, (MSb − MSw) / n₀)   (between-run SD)
SI = √(Sr² + Sb²)            (intermediate precision SD)

CV%_repeatability = (Sr / x̄) × 100
CV%_intermediate = (SI / x̄) × 100

SI is the clinically relevant figure — it captures both within-run and between-run variation and reflects day-to-day laboratory performance. Compare CV%_intermediate against your allowable imprecision goal (biological variation or manufacturer's claim).

If a variance component estimate is negative (a known artefact of the method), it is set to zero. All CVs are reported as % of the mean at that level.

EFLM biological variation goals

The tool integrates the EFLM Biological Variation Database. Selecting an analyte and tier auto-fills the allowable imprecision input based on:

Allowable CV ≤ f × 0.5 × CVᵢ

Where CVᵢ is within-subject biological variation and f = 0.25 (Optimal), 0.50 (Desirable), 0.75 (Minimum). The PASS / FAIL verdict compares your reproducibility CV against this goal.

EP15 verification

EP15 tests whether your observed precision is consistent with the manufacturer's claim at 95% confidence. The tool computes a one-sided chi-squared test:

χ² = (n − 1) × S²observed / S²claimed

PASS if χ² ≤ χ²(0.95, df). FAIL means the observed CV exceeds the claim at the 5% significance level.

Accuracy & limitations

Accuracy & limitations: Results are only valid if the study design is followed correctly — any deviation from the EP05 schedule (e.g., fewer days, fewer runs) reduces the reliability of variance component estimates. CV estimates have wide uncertainty at small sample sizes; the 95% CI on CV should be reported. EFLM goals are derived from published biological variation data and are updated periodically — verify against the current EFLM database for regulatory submissions. This tool is intended for planning, verification, and education; always follow your laboratory's accreditation requirements for formal validation documentation.
