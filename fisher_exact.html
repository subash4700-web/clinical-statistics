Accuracy & limitations: Fisher's Exact Test computes an exact p-value by enumerating all possible tables with the same marginal totals — no approximation is involved. It is most appropriate for small samples or when any expected cell count is below 5. With larger samples (all expected counts ≥ 5), chi-square gives equivalent results and is computationally faster. The test is conservative and two-sided p-values are computed by doubling the one-sided probability, which can be slightly conservative for asymmetric distributions.
