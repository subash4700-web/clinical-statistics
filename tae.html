Details & Concepts
InputCV% from precision study (per level) + Bias% from trueness or method comparison study + TAE% from EFLM, CLIA, or your specification
FormulaTE = |Bias%| + 1.65 × CV%  ·  Sigma = (TAE% − |Bias%|) / CV%
TAETotal Allowable Error — the maximum combined error (systematic + random) that a patient result may have before it affects clinical decisions. Set from EFLM biological variation, CLIA proficiency limits, or your laboratory's own quality goals.
SigmaSigma metric quantifies how much of the TAE budget is consumed by your method's bias and imprecision. Sigma ≥ 6 = world class. 4–6 = good. 3–4 = marginal — intensive QC needed. < 3 = poor — root-cause investigation required before release.
Two levelsCV% often differs between concentration levels — typically higher (%) at low concentrations. Both levels must independently meet TAE. Bias is assumed constant across levels unless proportional bias was found in method comparison (slope ≠ 1), in which case enter level-specific bias manually.
QC strategySigma drives your QC rule selection (Westgard). High sigma = simple rules, low frequency. Low sigma = tight multirules, frequent runs. The goal is to detect errors before they affect patient results.
OutputTE%, sigma metric per level, QC rule recommendation, patient result distribution chart
