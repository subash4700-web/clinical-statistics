How Kappa works

κ = (Po − Pe) / (1 − Pe)

Po = observed agreement = (a+d)/n

Pe = expected agreement by chance alone

Kappa corrects for chance — high Po with high Pe gives a low κ (the Kappa paradox).

McNemar tests whether b ≠ c (asymmetric disagreement).

If significant: one method calls more positives than the other — there is a systematic directional bias.
