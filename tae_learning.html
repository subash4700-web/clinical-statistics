⚖️ Understanding Total Error & Sigma

See how bias and imprecision combine into total error — and what the sigma metric tells you about method quality and QC design.

Exercise 1
World class — Sigma ≥ 6
Low bias, low imprecision. The distribution is well within TAE limits. Almost no patient results are unacceptable.
Bias = 0.5% CV = 0.8% TAE = 6%
Exercise 2
Good method — Sigma ~4–5
Acceptable performance. A small tail exceeds TAE. Standard QC rules are sufficient to catch errors.
Bias = 1.5% CV = 1.5% TAE = 8%
Exercise 3
Marginal — Sigma ~3
Method is on the edge. A visible proportion of results fall outside TAE. Tighter QC is required to maintain safety.
Bias = 2% CV = 2% TAE = 8%
Exercise 4
Poor method — Sigma < 3
Large proportion of patient results exceed TAE. QC alone cannot rescue this method — improvement is needed.
Bias = 3% CV = 3% TAE = 8%
Exercise 5
Fix the method
Start with a failing method. Adjust bias and CV until sigma reaches ≥ 4. Which matters more — reducing bias or improving precision?
Bias = 4% CV = 3% TAE = 10%
