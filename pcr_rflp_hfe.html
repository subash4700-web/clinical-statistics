Clinical Context

Hereditary Haemochromatosis (HH)

Autosomal recessive disorder of iron metabolism. Excess iron accumulates in liver, heart, and pancreas. The HFE C282Y mutation (p.Cys282Tyr) accounts for ~85% of cases in Northern European populations.

Molecular Mechanism

How does C282Y affect RsaI digestion?

The c.845G>A substitution changes the codon 282 from TGC (Cys) to TAC (Tyr). This single nucleotide change, combined with the flanking sequence, creates a new RsaI recognition site (GT↓AC) that is absent in the wild-type allele.

Wild-type: …GTGGCTA…  ← G at c.845 → GTGC — NOT an RsaI site
C282Y: …GTGACTA…  ← A at c.845 → GTAC — RsaI site created ✓
RsaI cuts between GT and AC:  …GT↓AC…
Wild-type allele (+)
1 constitutive RsaI site (always present)
→ 1 cut → 2 fragments: 90 bp + 76 bp
C282Y allele (m)
1 constitutive + 1 C282Y-specific site
→ 2 cuts → 3 fragments: 76 bp + 61 bp + 29 bp

Key Learning Points
1
PCR-RFLP principle: A restriction enzyme is used to distinguish alleles based on the presence or absence of its recognition sequence at the polymorphic site.
2
Constitutive site: The amplicon contains one RsaI site in ALL alleles (digestion control). This confirms the enzyme worked — if you see uncut 166 bp, digestion has failed.
3
C282Y creates a site: The c.845G>A change converts GTGC to GTAC, introducing a new RsaI recognition site. The mutant allele is therefore cut twice.
4
Heterozygous pattern: The 76 bp band appears with greater intensity in heterozygotes — it is produced by BOTH alleles (one from each), so it contains twice the amount of DNA.
5
Clinical note: C282Y homozygosity has ~80% penetrance for elevated iron markers but only ~10–33% penetrance for clinical haemochromatosis. Genotype alone does not confirm disease.
