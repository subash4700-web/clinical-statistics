Lambert-Beer law
A = ε · l · c
A — absorbance (dimensionless, –log₁₀ of transmittance)
ε — molar absorptivity (L·mol⁻¹·cm⁻¹), a constant for a given compound at a given wavelength
l — optical path length (cm), typically 1 cm for standard cuvettes
c — concentration (mol/L)
Accuracy & limitations: Lambert-Beer law is linear only within a certain absorbance range — typically A < 1.5 (ideally < 1.0). At high absorbance, detector saturation and stray light cause negative deviation from linearity. Always measure a reagent blank and subtract it. If the compound absorbs at the measurement wavelength, choose a wavelength at or near the absorption maximum (λmax) for maximum sensitivity.
