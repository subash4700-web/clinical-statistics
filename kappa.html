Diagonal cells (marked ⬤) show agreement between raters. Colors show if observed agreement exceeds chance expectations. Expected table shows what would occur by chance alone based on marginal distributions.
Accuracy & limitations: The κ value and its interpretation scale are standard outputs of Cohen's Kappa. The 95% confidence interval shown in the results is an asymptotic approximation — it is not an official part of the Kappa test itself, but is included as a supplementary indicator of precision. The CI should be interpreted cautiously with small samples (n < 30) or extreme marginal distributions. Kappa is also sensitive to prevalence: even high observed agreement can produce a low κ if one category is very rare. For ordinal categories where disagreement severity matters, use Weighted Kappa instead.
